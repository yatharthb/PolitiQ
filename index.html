Why this exists

Most people can name their president. Far fewer can name the senators and representatives whose votes shape daily life. We think that's fixable, and quizzes are a fun place to start.

1

Recognise the names

Familiarity comes first. The more often you see a name, the more easily you'll recognise it in news, hearings, and your own ballot.

2

Anchor them to a place

Every legislator answers to a state — and most to a district. Tying names to geography makes the whole map click.

3

Build from there

Future quizzes will cover Cabinet members, Supreme Court justices, governors, and key historical congresses.
